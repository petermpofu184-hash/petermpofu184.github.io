My Academic Journey

300–600 words reflection

Starting college has been both exciting and challenging for me. One of my biggest worries in the beginning was how I would socialize and make new friends. I had taken a gap year before starting university, so when I arrived, I didn’t know anyone. Walking into a new environment where everyone seemed to already know someone made me feel unsure and isolated. Slowly, I began interacting with a few classmates, and over time I learned that stepping out of my comfort zone was the only way to grow.

Another major challenge was time management. College is very different from high school—no one forces you to study, attend classes, or submit work on time. It is your responsibility. I struggled to balance attending lectures, completing assignments, studying, and still having personal time. I often found myself studying late at night or even at the last minute before deadlines. I realized that this was not because I was lazy, but because I needed to learn how to plan my time better and stay disciplined.

When it came to choosing my programme, things didn’t go exactly as planned. I grew up in a family that is passionate about engineering—mechanical, automotive, electrical, and even aerospace. Naturally, I wanted a career that combined all of these fields, so I first aimed for Mechatronics Engineering. However, life took a different turn and I ended up choosing Computer Systems Engineering. What attracted me was the word “systems”, because systems exist in every kind of engineering—mechanical systems, electrical systems, software systems. I realized that technology controls and connects all these fields, and studying systems engineering would still allow me to explore the kind of innovation I’ve always been interested in.

I study best at night because there is less noise and fewer distractions. The quiet allows me to focus better. However, I still struggle sometimes with leaving things until the last minute—so I know my time management still needs improvement. But I am working on it step by step.

When I think about achievements, I don’t feel like I have done anything very big yet. But I remind myself that sometimes small achievements still matter. I am proud that I made it to university, especially after taking a gap year. I am also proud that I managed to start my own online store, which taught me about responsibility, business, and independence.

Overall, my academic journey so far has taught me resilience, self-discipline, and patience. I am still growing, still learning, and still improving—but I am confident that this journey is shaping me into the person and professional I want to become.

Peter Junior Mpofu • CSE25-217
